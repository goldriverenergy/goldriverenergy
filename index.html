About GRECBS


History GRECBS was officially formed on the 18 June 2012 in Nepal, with the four associate partners of the UK organisation Concorde Business Services (CBS) together with four high ranking Nepal nationals representing both the political and business communities. The conception of its formation was built up over many months of consultation both in Nepal and UK examining the business opportunities in Nepal that would benefit Nepal, its people and investors. It soon became apparent that Nepal’s greatest asset was the Himalayas and the water from which it produces in abundance. Water is to become a very valuable commodity in the future as our planet’s population grows, some say it could become as valuable as gold is today. However the value of the Nepal water which is available now can help not only the people of Nepal in providing jobs and income but provides a ‘green’ inexhaustible supply of natural energy which can help many of its neighbouring countries with additional electricity they so badly need. This of course not only places Nepal in a strong economic position in Asia but also it provides them with income. GRECBS has been invited to provide hydro-electric power schemes of the ‘run-of-river’ type with our joint venture partner the Nepal Electricity Authority (NEA), who has already provided Feasibility Reports for their optimum locations and capacity of each of these schemes. The role of GRECBS in these ventures is to provide project investment, skilled consultants in engineering design, and overall project development in conjunction with NEA.


Objective Encouraging the investors of international sector and conducting research and study on the different types of minerals and raw materials, petroleum products, water resources and energy existing in Nepal, establish small and large industries taking permission from the concerning authorities and mobilize resources and means for overall development of the country. To research and investigate on different minerals and petroleum products, water resources along with energy and establish small as well as large electrification and sell the same, distribute and manage training and make treaties and agreements coordinating with governmental and non-governmental authorities, companies and investors of national and international sectors and mobilize resources and means remaining within the country and establish trading and maintenance centres for the related parts/machineries and provide all necessary works and services related to it. To research different medicinal plants, different minerals; gold, silver, iron, copper, lead along with other important metals as well as petroleum products found in different places in Nepal and establish mines and processing centres obtaining permission from concerned authorities.
